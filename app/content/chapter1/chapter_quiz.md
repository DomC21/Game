1. C - Higher risk investments generally offer higher potential returns, but also greater potential for loss. This is the fundamental risk-return tradeoff in investing.

2. C - Historically, stocks have provided the highest long-term returns among the major asset classes, despite experiencing significant short-term volatility.

3. B - $5,000 × (1 + 0.08)^20 = $23,305.84

4. C - Diversification primarily reduces unsystematic (company-specific) risk by spreading investments across multiple securities, sectors, and asset classes.

5. A - For long-term goals (20+ years), a higher allocation to stocks is typically appropriate to maximize growth potential, as there is sufficient time to recover from market downturns.

6. B - Roth IRAs offer tax-free growth and tax-free qualified withdrawals in retirement, unlike Traditional IRAs and 401(k)s which offer tax-deferred growth but taxable withdrawals.

7. C - Dollar-cost averaging removes the emotional aspect of trying to time the market by investing a fixed amount at regular intervals, regardless of market conditions.

8. B - The Efficient Market Hypothesis states that current market prices reflect all available information, making it difficult to consistently outperform the market.

9. B - Inflation reduces the purchasing power of investment returns, meaning that the real return (nominal return minus inflation) is what matters for maintaining purchasing power.

10. C - With a 0.1% expense ratio, you would have approximately $100,627; with a 1% expense ratio, you would have approximately $73,048, a difference of about $27,579.

11. B - Given Maria's age (30) and retirement goal (age 65), she has a 35-year time horizon, which supports a growth-oriented portfolio with 70-80% stocks.

12. C - $25,000 growing at 7% for 35 years plus $500 monthly contributions would grow to approximately $777,000.

13. B - A 529 College Savings Plan is specifically designed for education expenses, offering tax-free growth and withdrawals for qualified education expenses.

15. B - Maintaining a long-term strategy and rebalancing back to target allocations is typically the most appropriate response to market volatility for long-term investors.
