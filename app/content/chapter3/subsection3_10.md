# 3.10 Chart Patterns: Reversal

## Introduction
Reversal patterns are specific chart formations that signal a potential change in the direction of the prevailing trend. These patterns are crucial for traders to identify potential turning points in the market. This subsection explores the most common reversal patterns, their characteristics, how to identify them, and strategies for trading them effectively.

## Head and Shoulders Pattern

### Head and Shoulders Top
The head and shoulders top is one of the most reliable reversal patterns, signaling a potential change from an uptrend to a downtrend.

**Components:**
- **Left Shoulder**: A peak followed by a decline
- **Head**: A higher peak followed by another decline
- **Right Shoulder**: A lower peak (similar height to left shoulder)
- **Neckline**: Support line connecting the troughs between the shoulders and head

**Characteristics:**
- Forms after an established uptrend
- Volume typically highest on left shoulder, lower on head, lowest on right shoulder
- Neckline can be horizontal or slightly sloped
- Pattern completes when price breaks below the neckline

**Trading Strategy:**
1. Identify the pattern after an uptrend
2. Wait for a break below the neckline with increased volume
3. Enter short position after the breakout is confirmed
4. Set stop-loss above the right shoulder
5. Target at least the distance from the head to the neckline, measured from the breakout point

### Inverse Head and Shoulders
The inverse head and shoulders (or head and shoulders bottom) signals a potential change from a downtrend to an uptrend.

**Components:**
- **Left Shoulder**: A trough followed by a rally
- **Head**: A lower trough followed by another rally
- **Right Shoulder**: A higher trough (similar to left shoulder)
- **Neckline**: Resistance line connecting the peaks between the shoulders and head

**Characteristics:**
- Forms after an established downtrend
- Volume pattern mirrors the head and shoulders top
- Pattern completes when price breaks above the neckline

**Trading Strategy:**
1. Identify the pattern after a downtrend
2. Wait for a break above the neckline with increased volume
3. Enter long position after the breakout is confirmed
4. Set stop-loss below the right shoulder
5. Target at least the distance from the head to the neckline, measured from the breakout point

## Double Top and Double Bottom Patterns

### Double Top
The double top pattern resembles the letter "M" and signals a potential reversal from an uptrend to a downtrend.

**Characteristics:**
- Two distinct peaks at approximately the same price level
- A trough between the peaks (typically 10-20% below the peaks)
- Volume usually higher on the first peak than the second
- Pattern completes when price breaks below the trough between the peaks

**Trading Strategy:**
1. Identify two peaks at similar levels after an uptrend
2. Wait for a break below the trough between the peaks
3. Confirm with increased volume on the breakdown
4. Enter short position after confirmation
5. Set stop-loss above the second peak
6. Target at least the height of the pattern measured from the breakdown point

### Double Bottom
The double bottom pattern resembles the letter "W" and signals a potential reversal from a downtrend to an uptrend.

**Characteristics:**
- Two distinct troughs at approximately the same price level
- A peak between the troughs (typically 10-20% above the troughs)
- Volume usually higher on the first trough than the second
- Pattern completes when price breaks above the peak between the troughs

**Trading Strategy:**
1. Identify two troughs at similar levels after a downtrend
2. Wait for a break above the peak between the troughs
3. Confirm with increased volume on the breakout
4. Enter long position after confirmation
5. Set stop-loss below the second trough
6. Target at least the height of the pattern measured from the breakout point

## Triple Top and Triple Bottom Patterns

### Triple Top
The triple top is similar to the double top but with three peaks at approximately the same level.

**Characteristics:**
- Three distinct peaks at approximately the same price level
- Troughs between the peaks
- Volume typically diminishes with each peak
- Pattern completes when price breaks below the lowest trough

**Trading Strategy:**
1. Identify three peaks at similar levels after an uptrend
2. Wait for a break below the lowest trough
3. Confirm with increased volume on the breakdown
4. Enter short position after confirmation
5. Set stop-loss above the third peak
6. Target at least the height of the pattern measured from the breakdown point

### Triple Bottom
The triple bottom is similar to the double bottom but with three troughs at approximately the same level.

**Characteristics:**
- Three distinct troughs at approximately the same price level
- Peaks between the troughs
- Volume typically diminishes with each trough
- Pattern completes when price breaks above the highest peak

**Trading Strategy:**
1. Identify three troughs at similar levels after a downtrend
2. Wait for a break above the highest peak
3. Confirm with increased volume on the breakout
4. Enter long position after confirmation
5. Set stop-loss below the third trough
6. Target at least the height of the pattern measured from the breakout point

## Rounding Patterns

### Rounding Top (Rounding Over)
The rounding top, also known as a rounded reversal or saucer top, represents a gradual, curved change from an uptrend to a downtrend.

**Characteristics:**
- Gradual shift from bullish to bearish sentiment
- Forms a dome or inverted "U" shape
- Typically spans several weeks or months
- Volume often mirrors the price pattern, declining as the round top forms and increasing as price begins to fall

**Trading Strategy:**
1. Identify the pattern after an uptrend
2. Wait for the price to break below the right side of the pattern
3. Confirm with increasing volume on the breakdown
4. Enter short position after confirmation
5. Set stop-loss at recent swing high
6. Target at least the height of the pattern measured from the breakdown point

### Rounding Bottom (Rounding Under)
The rounding bottom, also known as a saucer bottom, represents a gradual, curved change from a downtrend to an uptrend.

**Characteristics:**
- Gradual shift from bearish to bullish sentiment
- Forms a bowl or "U" shape
- Typically spans several weeks or months
- Volume often mirrors the price pattern, declining as the round bottom forms and increasing as price begins to rise

**Trading Strategy:**
1. Identify the pattern after a downtrend
2. Wait for the price to break above the right side of the pattern
3. Confirm with increasing volume on the breakout
4. Enter long position after confirmation
5. Set stop-loss at recent swing low
6. Target at least the height of the pattern measured from the breakout point

## V-Bottoms and V-Tops

### V-Bottom
A V-bottom represents a sharp, sudden reversal from a downtrend to an uptrend without a transition period.

**Characteristics:**
- Steep, rapid decline followed by an equally steep rise
- Little or no consolidation at the bottom
- Often accompanied by a climactic volume spike at the reversal point
- Typically forms during panic selling or capitulation

**Trading Strategy:**
1. Identify the sharp reversal after a downtrend
2. Look for confirmation signals (bullish candlestick patterns, positive divergence)
3. Enter long position after confirmation
4. Set stop-loss below the V-bottom
5. Use Fibonacci retracement levels for potential targets

### V-Top
A V-top represents a sharp, sudden reversal from an uptrend to a downtrend without a transition period.

**Characteristics:**
- Steep, rapid rise followed by an equally steep decline
- Little or no consolidation at the top
- Often accompanied by a climactic volume spike at the reversal point
- Typically forms during euphoric buying or exhaustion

**Trading Strategy:**
1. Identify the sharp reversal after an uptrend
2. Look for confirmation signals (bearish candlestick patterns, negative divergence)
3. Enter short position after confirmation
4. Set stop-loss above the V-top
5. Use Fibonacci retracement levels for potential targets

## Diamond Patterns

### Diamond Top
The diamond pattern is a relatively rare reversal pattern that forms at market tops.

**Characteristics:**
- Begins with a broadening pattern (expanding range)
- Followed by a contracting pattern (narrowing range)
- Forms a diamond shape
- Volume typically decreases as the pattern develops
- Pattern completes when price breaks below the lower trendline

**Trading Strategy:**
1. Identify the diamond pattern after an uptrend
2. Wait for a break below the lower trendline
3. Confirm with increased volume on the breakdown
4. Enter short position after confirmation
5. Set stop-loss above the most recent swing high
6. Target at least the height of the pattern measured from the breakdown point

### Diamond Bottom
The diamond bottom is the inverse of the diamond top and forms at market bottoms.

**Characteristics:**
- Begins with a broadening pattern (expanding range)
- Followed by a contracting pattern (narrowing range)
- Forms a diamond shape
- Volume typically decreases as the pattern develops
- Pattern completes when price breaks above the upper trendline

**Trading Strategy:**
1. Identify the diamond pattern after a downtrend
2. Wait for a break above the upper trendline
3. Confirm with increased volume on the breakout
4. Enter long position after confirmation
5. Set stop-loss below the most recent swing low
6. Target at least the height of the pattern measured from the breakout point

## Reversal Patterns vs. Continuation Patterns

### Key Differences
Understanding the differences between reversal and continuation patterns is crucial for accurate interpretation:

1. **Context**: Reversal patterns form after established trends; continuation patterns form during trend pauses
2. **Structure**: Reversal patterns often show more dramatic shifts in price structure
3. **Volume**: Reversal patterns typically show more significant volume characteristics
4. **Completion Time**: Reversal patterns often take longer to form than continuation patterns
5. **Reliability**: Major reversal patterns (H&S, double tops/bottoms) tend to be more reliable than minor ones

### Pattern Reliability Factors
Several factors influence the reliability of reversal patterns:

1. **Prior Trend Duration**: Patterns forming after longer trends are more significant
2. **Pattern Size**: Larger patterns (in terms of price range and time) are more reliable
3. **Volume Confirmation**: Clear volume patterns increase reliability
4. **Market Context**: Patterns aligned with broader market conditions are more reliable
5. **Multiple Timeframe Confirmation**: Patterns visible across multiple timeframes are stronger

## Measuring Targets for Reversal Patterns

### The Measured Move Method
The measured move is a technique for estimating price targets after a pattern breakout:

1. **Head and Shoulders**: Measure the vertical distance from the head to the neckline, then project this distance from the neckline breakpoint
2. **Double/Triple Tops and Bottoms**: Measure the height from the peaks to the trough (or troughs to the peak), then project this distance from the breakout point
3. **Rounding Patterns**: Measure the vertical distance from the highest to lowest point, then project this from the breakout point
4. **Diamond Patterns**: Measure the widest part of the diamond, then project this distance from the breakout point

### Alternative Target Methods
**Fibonacci Extensions:**
- Use 127.2%, 161.8%, or 261.8% extensions from the pattern's range

**Previous Support/Resistance:**
- Identify key levels beyond the pattern that may act as targets

**Trendline Projections:**
- Extend trendlines to identify potential target areas

## Key Takeaways
- Reversal patterns signal potential changes in the direction of the prevailing trend
- Common reversal patterns include head and shoulders, double/triple tops and bottoms, rounding patterns, and diamond patterns
- Volume confirmation is crucial for validating reversal patterns
- The measured move technique helps establish price targets after breakouts
- Reversal patterns are more reliable when they form after extended trends and are confirmed by volume
- Combining reversal patterns with other technical tools improves trading success
